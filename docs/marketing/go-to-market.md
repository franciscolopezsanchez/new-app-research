## 1. GTM Overview

### The Core Problem We Are Solving

Every private guardería in Spain currently "communicates" via three broken tools: a WhatsApp group the director hates but tolerates, paper notes that parents lose, and teachers answering messages at 11pm on their personal phones. No school director woke up this morning thinking "I need a parent communication app." They woke up thinking "I need parents to stop calling me directly," "I need teachers to stop burning out," and "I need to know that a LOPDGDD audit won't cost me €10,000."

Our GTM is not about selling software. It is about selling professional peace of mind to directors who are running small businesses on tight margins and zero administrative bandwidth.

### The One-Line Sales Pitch (Adapt Per Persona)

**To the director:** "Replace the WhatsApp group with something that protects your teachers, impresses parents, and keeps LOPDGDD inspectors happy — for less than a cup of coffee per child per month."

**To the teacher:** "You'll spend 3 minutes on the daily report instead of 15, and parents stop messaging you on your personal phone."

**To the parent:** "You get a photo of your kid every day. You never have to wonder."

---

### Phase 1: Pilot Acquisition (Months 0–6)
**Goal:** 10 paying schools, €4,900 MRR, 5 case studies in hand.

The entire focus is manual, founder-led sales. No marketing spend. The founder calls and emails directors directly using the school directory. We sign up 5 "Escuelas Fundadoras" (free for 12 months) and convert 5 more to paid. Every school is treated as a design partner, not just a customer. We learn what actually drives adoption.

**Key milestones:**
- Week 2: First demo call booked
- Week 6: First pilot school live
- Month 3: 5 pilots active, product validated
- Month 6: 10 schools total (5 paying), first testimonials

### Phase 2: Traction (Months 6–18)
**Goal:** 100 paying schools, ~€5,500 MRR (blended), referral engine starting to work.

Outreach becomes more systematic. We hire or contract a part-time sales development rep (SDR) to run email sequences. Content SEO starts generating inbound leads. Word-of-mouth between schools in the same city starts closing deals without outreach. We present at one regional education event.

**Key milestones:**
- Month 9: 30 paying schools
- Month 12: 60 paying schools, first referral program launched
- Month 18: 100 paying schools, 2–3 association partnerships active

### Phase 3: Scale (Months 18–36)
**Goal:** 500+ paying schools, LATAM validation, possible chain/franchise sales motion.

Inbound is a meaningful percentage of new leads. We have case studies for every school segment. We begin exploring Mexico City and Buenos Aires as first LATAM markets (similar regulatory pressure, same language, large private preschool sectors). Chain and franchise outreach (e.g., Nemomarlin, Bambinos) becomes its own sales track.

**Key milestones:**
- Month 24: 250 paying schools
- Month 30: First LATAM pilot (Mexico or Argentina)
- Month 36: 500+ paying schools, Series A or sustainable profitability

## 2. Target Segmentation

### Priority Segment: Who We Target First

**Segment A (Primary): Private guarderías, 20–60 children, Madrid and Catalunya**
- School type: Private (not concertado, not public)
- Size: 20–60 children (too big for free tier, small enough that one director makes all decisions)
- Location: Madrid city, Barcelona city, and surrounding metropolitan areas
- Decision maker: Director who is often also owner
- Why: Highest willingness to pay, no bureaucratic budget approval, GDPR awareness is highest in Madrid and Catalunya (more inspections, more business-oriented directors), school density means geographic clustering for word-of-mouth

**Segment B (Secondary, from Month 3): Concertado-privado guarderías**
- Partially publicly funded but privately run
- Slightly more price-sensitive but still have director-level purchasing authority
- Good for volume in months 3–12 once we have proof points

**Segment C (Growth, from Month 6): Private guarderías in Valencia, Basque Country, Galicia**
- Valencia: Large market, high private guardería density, no regional language barrier
- Basque Country: High income per capita, compliance-aware, Euskera support is a hard differentiator
- Galicia: Less competitive, Galician support differentiates strongly vs ClassDojo

### Do NOT Target Initially

**Public schools (escuelas infantiles municipales):**
- Budget controlled by municipal government, not director
- 6–18 month procurement cycles
- Requires formal tender (licitación pública) above certain thresholds
- Teachers are civil servants — change management is extremely hard
- Come back in Phase 3 with a dedicated public sector motion

**Large chains (Nemomarlin, Bambinos, Kinderland, etc.):**
- Purchasing is centralized at HQ, not school level
- Long enterprise sales cycles (6–12 months minimum)
- Require custom contracts, procurement reviews, SLAs
- Distract founder from learning fast
- Exception: If a chain director reaches out to us inbound, do not ignore them — but do not prioritize outbound to chains in Phase 1

**Schools already using a paid app (KinderClose, etc.):**
- Switching cost is high (data migration, retraining)
- They already believe in the category — come back when we have a head-to-head comparison case study
- Assumption to validate: What percentage of target schools already use any paid app? Estimate: less than 5%

### Geographic Prioritization Logic

| Community | Priority | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Madrid | 1 | Highest school density, most business-oriented directors, highest compliance awareness, founder likely based here |
| Catalunya | 1 | High density, strong GDPR culture, Catalan language support is hard differentiator vs ClassDojo |
| Valencia | 2 | Large market, no language barrier, good density |
| Basque Country | 2 | High income, compliance aware, Euskera = unique differentiator |
| Galicia | 3 | Galician support differentiates, lower volume but loyal |
| Andalucía | 3 | Huge market but lower compliance awareness, more price-sensitive, tackle in Phase 2 |
| Everywhere else | 4 | Respond to inbound, do not prioritize outbound until Phase 2 |

**Assumption to validate:** Are there more private guarderías per capita in Madrid vs Barcelona? Pull the directory data and count before deciding where to start.

## 3. Cold Outreach Strategy

### Overview

The public school directory (names, addresses, phone numbers, emails of directors) is our single most valuable asset right now. Used correctly, it is a 6-month head start over any competitor who has to build this list from scratch.

The goal of every outreach touch is singular: **book a 20-minute demo call or activate a free trial.** Not to explain the product. Not to send a brochure. Not to get feedback. Get them on a call or get them to sign up.

---

### 3.1 Email Outreach

#### Pre-Send Hygiene
- Filter the directory to private and concertado-privado only (exclude colegios públicos, CEIPs)
- Verify emails are valid (run through a bulk verifier — tools: NeverBounce, ZeroBounce)
- Segment by city so you can personalize the city name in the email
- Start with Madrid batch first, then Barcelona

#### Sending Infrastructure
- Do NOT use your personal Gmail. It will get flagged and your domain will be blacklisted.
- Use a dedicated sending domain (e.g., comunica-guarderia.es or similar) separate from your main product domain
- Tool recommendation: Instantly.ai or Lemlist (both have Spanish-language support and warm-up features)
- Warm up the domain for 2 weeks before sending volume
- Send limit: Max 50 emails/day per mailbox. Use 2–3 mailboxes to send 100–150/day.
- Send Tuesday–Thursday, 8:30am–10:00am local time (before the school day chaos starts)

#### Subject Line A/B Test (rotate across three variants)

**Variant A (Compliance angle — for directors aware of GDPR):**
```
¿Tu guardería usa WhatsApp para comunicarse con las familias?
```

**Variant B (Teacher burnout angle — emotional):**
```
Tus educadoras reciben mensajes de padres a las 10 de la noche
```

**Variant C (Direct value / curiosity):**
```
Lo que usan las guarderías de [CIUDAD] en lugar de WhatsApp
```

Track open rates per variant. Expect 20–35% open rates for cold email in this sector if subject lines are good. Below 15% means deliverability problem, not content problem.

#### Follow-Up Sequence

The first email rarely converts. The sequence is where deals are won.

**Day 0:** Send initial email (one of the three variants above)

**Day 3 (Follow-up 1):** Short, no re-pitch

```
Asunto: Re: [hilo anterior]

Hola [Nombre],

Solo quería asegurarme de que mi email anterior te llegó bien.
¿Tienes un momento esta semana?

[Nombre]
```

**Day 7 (Follow-up 2):** Add a new angle (value or urgency)

```
Asunto: Una pregunta rápida sobre [Nombre del centro]

Hola [Nombre],

Entiendo que estás muy ocupada. Solo una pregunta: ¿usáis actualmente alguna app de comunicación con las familias, o seguís con WhatsApp?

Si ya tenéis algo que funciona, no quiero molestarte. Si no, creo que tengo algo que te puede ahorrar mucho tiempo a ti y a tus educadoras.

¿Me puedes decir en una línea cómo gestionáis la comunicación ahora mismo?

[Nombre]
```

**Day 14 (Follow-up 3):** The breakup email — highest response rate in most cold sequences

```
Asunto: Último mensaje de mi parte

Hola [Nombre],

Sé que estos meses son muy intensos en las guarderías. No quiero seguir llenándote la bandeja de entrada.

Este es mi último email. Si en algún momento quieres ver cómo estamos ayudando a otras guarderías en [CIUDAD] a dejar el WhatsApp, aquí me tienes: [email] o [teléfono].

Mucha suerte con el centro.

[Nombre]
```

**Total sequence:** 4 touches over 14 days. After that, move to "low-touch nurture" (monthly newsletter) — do not keep cold-emailing unresponsive contacts.

**Expected conversion rate (cold email to demo booked):** 2–4% of emails sent. At 100 emails/day, that is 2–4 demo calls booked per day, which is more than you can handle in Month 1. Start with 30–50/day and ramp up.

### 3.2 Phone Outreach

Phone converts 3–5x better than email for this persona. Directors are rarely at their computers. They are in the school. A direct call gets an answer that email never will.

**When to call:** Tuesday–Thursday, 9:30–11:00am or 3:00–5:00pm. Avoid Monday mornings (chaos) and Friday afternoons (mentally checked out).

**Caller ID:** Use a local number (Madrid for Madrid calls, Barcelona for Barcelona calls). Google Voice or a VoIP service with local area codes. Directors screen unknown numbers but are more likely to pick up a local prefix.

#### Phone Script

```
RING...

[Director answers]

"Buenos días, ¿hablo con [Nombre]? Soy [Tu nombre], llamo de [App name],
una aplicación de comunicación para guarderías.

Te llamo porque hemos estado trabajando con varias guarderías en [CIUDAD]
para ayudarles a dejar el WhatsApp — y pensé que podría interesarte.

¿Tienes 2 minutos ahora mismo, o te pillo en mal momento?"

[If yes, continue:]

"Perfecto. Rápido: ¿cómo gestionáis ahora mismo la comunicación con
las familias — fotos, reportes del día, mensajes?"

[Listen. Let them talk. They will tell you everything you need to know.]

[If they say WhatsApp:]

"Sí, es lo más habitual. El problema que nos dicen los directores es que
las educadoras acaban con WhatsApp personal, y que técnicamente no
cumple la LOPDGDD para compartir imágenes de los niños.

Lo que hacemos nosotros es muy sencillo: las educadoras mandan el reporte
en 3 minutos, las familias reciben las fotos en una app cerrada, y nadie
da su número personal.

¿Tendría sentido que te hiciera una demo de 20 minutos? Te lo muestro
en pantalla, sin compromiso, y tú decides si encaja."

[Handle objections — see Section 3.4]

[Close:]

"¿Te viene bien el [DÍA] a las [HORA]? Te mando la invitación ahora
mismo a [email]."
```

**Key discipline:** Do not pitch the product over the phone. The only goal of the call is to book the 20-minute demo. As soon as they say yes to the demo, stop talking and confirm the slot.

**Volume target:** 20–30 calls per day when in outreach mode. Expect to reach 30–40% of numbers (the rest go to voicemail or are wrong numbers). Of those reached, target 10–15% demo conversion, so roughly 1–2 demos booked per 20–30 calls.

**Voicemail script (leave only on first attempt, never on follow-ups):**

```
"Buenos días [Nombre], soy [Tu nombre] de [App name]. Te llamo porque
trabajamos con guarderías en [CIUDAD] para gestionar la comunicación
con familias sin WhatsApp — cumpliendo la LOPDGDD. Si te interesa
saber más, puedes escribirme a [email] o llamarme al [número].
Que tengas un buen día."
```

### 3.3 Direct Mail / Physical Letter

**Should you do it? Yes — but only for your top 50–100 targets, not for bulk outreach.**

Physical mail has near-100% open rates in a world of email overload. A well-designed letter on quality paper sitting on a director's desk is a pattern interrupt. It signals that you are serious, not just another SaaS email blast.

**Use case:** Send physical letters to your highest-priority targets (largest private schools in Madrid and Barcelona) as the first touch, before email. Then follow up with email referencing the letter.

**Format:**
- A5 card, good quality paper stock, handwritten font (not actually handwritten — use a handwriting font for scale)
- Envelope addressed by hand or with a printed label that looks personal
- No brochure, no QR code maze — one clear message and one clear next step

**Letter copy:**

```
[Nombre del centro],
[Dirección],
[Ciudad]


Estimada [Nombre],

Le escribo a mano porque lo que quiero contarle merece más que un email.

Hemos construido la primera aplicación de comunicación diseñada
específicamente para guarderías españolas — no una traducción de
un producto americano, sino algo hecho desde cero para cumplir la
LOPDGDD y adaptarse a cómo trabajan los centros como el suyo.

Las educadoras de las guarderías que ya la usan dedican 3 minutos
al día al reporte. Las familias reciben fotos de sus hijos sin WhatsApp
personal. Y el centro tiene un registro documentado de todo.

Me encantaría mostrárselo en 20 minutos.

Puede llamarme directamente al [número] o escribirme a [email].

Con mucho respeto por el trabajo que hacéis,

[Tu nombre]
[App name]
```

**Follow-up:** 3–4 days after the letter should arrive, send the email sequence referencing it: "Le mandé una carta la semana pasada..."

**Cost:** ~€1.50–2.50 per letter including printing and postage. For 100 letters: €150–250. Worth it for top targets.

### 3.4 Objection Handling

#### "Ya usamos ClassDojo"

```
"Entiendo, ClassDojo es bastante conocido. ¿Sabéis que es una empresa
americana y que sus servidores están en EEUU? Para imágenes de menores
de 6 años, eso puede ser un problema con la LOPDGDD — los datos tienen
que procesarse en la UE. Nosotros cumplimos eso desde el primer día.
¿Os han preguntado alguna vez las familias dónde se almacenan las fotos
de sus hijos?"
```

#### "Usamos WhatsApp y funciona bien"

```
"Sí, funciona para comunicarse. La pregunta es si funciona para el centro.
¿Vuestras educadoras dan su número personal? ¿Reciben mensajes fuera
del horario escolar? ¿Tenéis documentado el consentimiento de cada
familia para recibir imágenes de los niños?

No digo que WhatsApp sea malo — digo que tiene un coste oculto
en tiempo y en riesgo legal que muchos directores no ven hasta
que tienen una queja de una familia o una inspección.

¿Me dejas mostrarte cómo lo hacemos nosotros? 20 minutos."
```

#### "No tenemos presupuesto"

```
"Entiendo que el presupuesto es ajustado — lo es para todas las guarderías.
Déjame preguntarte una cosa: ¿cuánto tiempo dedica cada educadora
al día a mandar fotos por WhatsApp, contestar mensajes de familias
y hacer los reportes en papel?

Si son 20–30 minutos al día por educadora, con 4 educadoras son
dos horas al día. Nuestra app cuesta menos que una hora de trabajo
al mes.

Y si todavía no veis el encaje, tenemos un plan gratuito para centros
pequeños. ¿Cuántos niños tenéis matriculados?"
```

#### "Ahora no es buen momento, llámame en septiembre"

```
"Perfectamente, lo entiendo. Solo una cosa: si queréis tenerlo listo
para el inicio del curso en septiembre, lo ideal es empezar a probarlo
en mayo o junio con un grupo pequeño de familias, para que las
educadoras lleguen al verano con confianza en la herramienta.

¿Me dejas mandarte un email con la información para que lo tengáis
en mente cuando hagáis la planificación de septiembre?"
```

### 3.5 The Conversion Goal

Every outreach interaction has one of three success outcomes:
1. **Book a 20-minute demo call** (preferred — highest conversion to paid)
2. **Activate a free trial** (acceptable — they self-discover value)
3. **Get permission to send more info** (minimum — keep warm for future)

Do not count "they said it sounds interesting" as a success. Count only calendar invites sent or trial accounts created.

## 4. Pilot School Program: Escuelas Fundadoras

### Program Concept

The "Escuelas Fundadoras" program is not a discount program. It is a co-design partnership. You are offering something scarce — the chance to shape a product from the ground up — to a small group of schools who will become your most powerful salespeople.

Frame it as an invitation, not a sales pitch. "We are selecting 8 schools in Spain to be our founding partners. We are not offering this to everyone."

### What Pilot Schools Get

- **Free access for 12 full months** — no credit card, no surprise invoice
- **Founding School badge** on their profile and on our website ("Escuela Fundadora")
- **Direct line to the founder** — WhatsApp or email, 48-hour response guaranteed
- **Monthly 30-minute product call** — their feedback shapes the roadmap
- **First access to every new feature** before general release
- **Co-creation credit** — their school name in release notes when a feature they requested ships
- **Price lock** — when they convert to paid after 12 months, they keep a founding-partner price permanently (e.g., 20% below standard pricing)

### What We Get From Pilot Schools

Be explicit about this in the agreement — it avoids awkwardness later.

- **Candid product feedback** every month (what is broken, what is missing, what is confusing)
- **Usage data** — we monitor activation, daily active use, feature adoption
- **1 written testimonial** after Month 3 (if they are genuinely happy)
- **1 case study interview** after Month 6 (30-minute recorded conversation)
- **3 warm referrals** — they introduce us to 3 other directors they know (not a cold lead, a warm intro)
- **Permission to use their logo and results** in sales materials

### Pilot School Selection Criteria

Not every school that says yes should be in the founding program. Be selective.

**Green flags (prioritize these):**
- Director is personally motivated and curious about the problem (not just polite)
- School has 20–50 children (big enough to be meaningful, small enough for fast decisions)
- Director is willing to commit teachers to the onboarding process
- School has a real pain point with WhatsApp that they verbalize unprompted
- Located in Madrid or Barcelona (easier for in-person visits, reference value is higher)

**Red flags (avoid these for the pilot):**
- Director says "sure, whatever" without asking a single question
- School already has a contract with another vendor
- Director says they need to "check with the governing board" for a free trial
- School has high staff turnover (product adoption will fail)
- Very small school (under 15 children) — they belong on the free tier, not the pilot program

### Pilot Program Structure (Per School)

**Week 1: Onboarding**
- Founder does a live 60-minute onboarding call with director + 1–2 teachers
- Set up all teacher accounts and at least 5 parent accounts together, live
- Agree on one specific workflow to start with (e.g., just daily activity reports, no messaging yet)
- Define "what success looks like in 30 days" together

**Month 1: Stabilization**
- Check-in call at Day 14: Is anyone confused? Are teachers actually using it daily?
- Identify the "power user teacher" — the one who loves it — and make them the internal champion
- Fix any critical bugs within 48 hours

**Month 3: Review**
- Full review call: What is working, what is not, what do they wish existed
- Request written testimonial if NPS is 8+
- Begin planning the case study

**Month 6: Case Study**
- 30-minute recorded conversation with director
- Ask: before/after comparison, specific numbers if possible (time saved, parent satisfaction)
- Publish as a case study on the website (with their consent and review)

**Month 12: Conversion**
- 30 days before the free year ends, have a commercial conversation
- Do not surprise them with an invoice — prepare them from Month 9 onward
- Offer the permanent founding-partner pricing
- Request the 3 warm referrals as a condition of the discounted price (frame it as a trade, not a demand)

### Target Numbers

| Period | Escuelas Fundadoras Target |
|---|---|
| Month 1 | 3 schools live |
| Month 3 | 8 schools live |
| Month 6 | 8 schools still active (churn target: 0) |
| Month 12 | 6 of 8 convert to paid (75% conversion target) |

## 5. Channel Strategy

### Channel 1: Direct Outreach (School Directory)

**Goal:** 10 paying schools in 6 months, 60 paying schools in 18 months.

**Effort level:** Very high (founder-led in Phase 1, SDR-assisted from Month 6).

**Expected timeline to first result:** 2–3 weeks to first demo, 4–6 weeks to first pilot.

**Key actions:**
- Build a clean, segmented version of the directory in a CRM (Notion, Airtable, or HubSpot free tier)
- Tag each school by size estimate (large/medium/small based on available data), type (private/concertado), and city
- Run email sequences as described in Section 3
- Phone outreach for top-priority targets
- Physical letters for top 100 targets in Madrid and Barcelona
- Track every contact, every touch, every outcome in the CRM — this data is irreplaceable

**Key metrics to watch:**
- Emails sent per week
- Email open rate (target: 25%+)
- Email reply rate (target: 5%+)
- Demo calls booked per week
- Demo-to-pilot conversion rate (target: 50%)
- Pilot-to-paid conversion rate (target: 60%)

## 6. Pricing and Conversion Strategy

### Free Tier Design

**What is included (always free):**
- Up to 15 children enrolled
- All core features: messaging, daily activity reports, photo/video sharing, attendance
- Full LOPDGDD compliance features (consent management, data residency in EU)
- iOS and Android apps for parents
- Web dashboard for teachers
- Standard support (email, 48-hour response)

**What is gated (paid only):**
- More than 15 children
- Multi-classroom management (more than 2 classrooms)
- Analytics and reporting dashboard for directors
- Priority support (same-day response)
- Custom branding (school logo in parent app)
- Bulk data export (GDPR right-to-portability compliance feature)
- API access (future)

**Free tier rationale:** We want every guardería in Spain to have access to the free tier permanently. Small schools (under 15 children, often family-run) should not be pressured to pay. But they will tell other directors about us, and those directors likely have larger schools. The free tier is a marketing channel, not a business model compromise.

**Assumption to validate:** What percentage of Spanish private guarderías have more than 15 children? Estimate: 70–80%. If correct, the free tier does not cannibalize paid revenue significantly.

### Trial vs Freemium: Which Model for Spain?

**Recommendation: Freemium, not time-limited trial.**

Time-limited trials work when the product has a clear, fast activation loop and the user experiences value within days. For guarderías, the sales cycle involves the director, teachers, and parents — getting all three groups onboarded takes 2–3 weeks. A 14-day trial expires before the school has even finished setup.

Freemium solves this. Schools get permanent access to the free tier. They experience value with no time pressure. When they hit the 15-child limit (which most will, because they are a paying school), the upgrade conversation is natural: "You have 23 children — to add the other 8, you need the standard plan."

**The upgrade trigger is a feature gate, not a deadline.** This is psychologically much easier for budget-constrained directors to accept.

**Exception:** Offer a 30-day "paid plan free trial" to schools that want to test premium features before committing. This is different from the free tier — it is a temporary unlock of all features with a clear end date.

### Demo-to-Paid Conversion Playbook

**Step 1: Pre-demo preparation**
- Research the school before the call (number of children if visible on their website, any news, their current communication method if mentioned anywhere)
- Prepare a demo environment with the school's name pre-loaded ("I've set this up as if it were [Nombre del centro]")
- Send a calendar invite with a reminder 24h and 1h before

**Step 2: The 20-minute demo structure**

| Time | Content |
|---|---|
| 0–3 min | Understand their current situation: "¿Cómo gestionáis ahora la comunicación con familias?" |
| 3–8 min | Show the teacher daily report flow (the biggest time-saver — this is the "aha" moment) |
| 8–13 min | Show the parent app (what the parent sees — emotional impact) |
| 13–17 min | Show LOPDGDD compliance features and consent management |
| 17–20 min | Pricing and next steps |

**Do not show everything.** Show the three things that matter most to this specific school based on what they told you in the first 3 minutes.

**Step 3: The close**

Do not end the demo without a next step. One of these three:
1. "¿Empezamos hoy con el plan gratuito? Os configuro yo mismo en 15 minutos."
2. "¿Os apuntáis como Escuela Fundadora? Solo me queda una plaza en Madrid."
3. "¿Empezamos con un periodo de prueba de 30 días del plan estándar?"

**Step 4: Post-demo follow-up**

Send within 2 hours of the call:

```
Asunto: Resumen de nuestra conversación + próximos pasos

Hola [Nombre],

Ha sido un placer hablar contigo. Tal como hemos comentado:

[2–3 bullet points summarizing what they said their main pains are]

Lo que te propongo:
[Specific next step you agreed on]

Si tienes alguna pregunta antes, estoy aquí.

Un abrazo,
[Nombre]
```

**Expected demo-to-paid conversion rate:** 30–40% (higher than typical SaaS because we are selling to a very specific, qualified persona with a real pain point and we are the only Spain-specific solution).

### Handling "We Don't Have Budget" at Small Guarderías

This objection usually means one of three things:
1. They genuinely cannot afford it
2. They are not convinced of the value
3. Budget is controlled by someone else

**Tactical responses:**

For schools under 15 children: "Perfecto — el plan gratuito es para siempre para centros de vuestro tamaño. ¿Empezamos hoy?"

For schools over 15 children who say no budget: "Entiendo. ¿Cuántos niños tenéis? Con [N] niños, son [€X] al mes — eso es [€X / N] por niño al mes, menos que una barra de pan. ¿El WhatsApp actual os cuesta más que eso en tiempo de las educadoras?"

For concertado schools where budget is approved by committee: "¿Cuándo tenéis la próxima reunión de equipo directivo? Os preparo un one-pager con el coste y la justificación para que lo tengáis listo."

## 8. Risks and Mitigation

### Risk 1: WhatsApp Inertia — Director Agrees, Teachers Won't Adopt

**Probability:** High. This is the most likely failure mode in Phase 1.

**What it looks like:** A director signs up enthusiastically. She announces it to her teachers. Two teachers use it, two don't. Parents get confused because some teachers use the app and others still use WhatsApp. Within 30 days, the director quietly reverts to WhatsApp.

**Root cause:** The director is the buyer but not the daily user. Teachers (Laura persona) will only adopt a tool that is faster than what they do today. If onboarding is confusing, if the app is slow, if it takes more than 3 minutes to do the daily report — they will reject it.

**Mitigation:**
- Do the onboarding with teachers present, not just the director
- Identify one "champion teacher" per school and give them extra attention
- Make the first 7 days of usage the product team's top priority (monitor daily, fix bugs immediately)
- Build a "teacher onboarding in 10 minutes" video guide in plain Spanish, no jargon
- Create a WhatsApp group (yes, ironically) with all teachers from pilot schools for peer support
- Track weekly active teachers in every pilot school — if it drops below 80%, call the director immediately
- Consider a "30-day teacher adoption guarantee": if teachers are not using it in 30 days, we will do an additional free onboarding session

---

### Risk 2: Budget Timing — Sales Cycle Locked to September Academic Year

**Probability:** High. Most guarderías plan their budgets and make operational decisions in August–September for the new academic year starting in September.

**What it looks like:** You launch outreach in April. Directors are interested but say "call me in September." Your pipeline fills up with interested but uncommitted leads. Conversion is low all summer. Then in September–October everyone wants to start at once and you are overwhelmed with onboarding.

**Mitigation:**
- Understand the academic calendar and align your outreach peaks accordingly:
  - **January–February:** Good time to plant seeds (new year energy, directors thinking about improvements)
  - **March–May:** Best outreach window (post-carnival, pre-summer planning mode)
  - **June–July:** Last chance before summer — "start now, be ready for September"
  - **August:** Dead zone. Do not send cold emails in August.
  - **September–October:** Conversion window — close all the leads from spring
- For leads that say "call in September": put them in a nurture sequence (one email per month, low-touch) and set a CRM reminder for September 1st
- Design the product so that schools can onboard in September and go live in October — build fast, easy setup into the product roadmap
- Consider a "summer setup" offer: sign up now, we do full setup in August while it is quiet, you are live on Day 1 of the new school year

---

### Risk 3: ClassDojo Drops Price or Adds Spanish Compliance Features

**Probability:** Low in Year 1, Medium in Year 2–3 (if we are growing visibly).

**What it looks like:** ClassDojo announces a GDPR/LOPDGDD compliance update and a Spanish-language tier priced at €29/month. Our primary differentiator is neutralized overnight.

**Mitigation:**
- Build defensibility beyond compliance. Compliance is the door opener, but it is not a sustainable moat. The moat is:
  - Deep product fit for 0–3 care context (not classroom management)
  - Regional language support (Catalan, Euskera, Galician) — ClassDojo will never prioritize this
  - Spanish customer support (someone who answers the phone in Spanish, who understands how guarderías work)
  - Community and network effects between Spanish schools
  - Price anchoring: €49 for a Spanish-specific product beats a US product at €49 because of trust, language, and support
- Build a community of Spanish guardería directors around the product — once they know and trust us, a compliance update from ClassDojo will not move them
- Continuously interview churned leads who chose ClassDojo — understand exactly what drove their decision and close that gap

---

### Risk 4: Low Parent Activation Kills Teacher Motivation

**Probability:** Medium. This is an underappreciated adoption failure mode.

**What it looks like:** Teachers use the app, send daily reports, post photos. But only 40% of parents actually install the app and engage. Teachers feel like they are doing extra work with no feedback. After 60 days they stop posting.

**Mitigation:**
- Make parent onboarding the director's responsibility, not the teachers'
- Provide a ready-made welcome message template directors can send to parents via WhatsApp at launch ("A partir de hoy, todas las novedades de vuestros hijos en...")
- Build a parent activation funnel: Day 1 SMS to parent, Day 3 push notification, Day 7 email — all automated
- Track parent activation rate (target: 70%+ within 14 days) as a leading indicator of school health
- If parent activation is below 50% at Day 14, trigger a support outreach to the director

---

### Risk 5: Founder Burnout from Solo Sales + Product Build

**Probability:** High for a technical founder doing cold sales simultaneously.

**What it looks like:** The founder is doing product development, cold calling, demos, onboarding, customer support, and content marketing simultaneously. Quality drops in all areas. First pilots get poor onboarding. Product bugs go unaddressed. Outreach volume drops.

**Mitigation:**
- Time-box sales activities strictly: 3 hours of outreach per day (morning), product development in the afternoon. Do not blur these blocks.
- Hire or contract a part-time sales assistant from Month 3 (not an SDR — an assistant who can handle email sequences and CRM hygiene, so the founder can focus on demo calls and onboarding)
- Define a "minimum viable sales day": 20 emails sent, 5 calls attempted, 1 demo completed — if this happens, the day was a success
- Accept that product development velocity will be slower in Phase 1. That is correct. You need customers more than you need features right now.
- Consider a co-founder with sales/commercial background — this is the single highest-leverage hire if accessible
