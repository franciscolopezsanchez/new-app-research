# Government Funding for Spanish Guarderías — Kit Digital and Alternatives

**Research date:** April 2, 2026
**Scope:** National and regional subsidy programs Spanish guarderías (centros de educación infantil 0-6) can use to fund a school-parent communication SaaS (~€59/month)

---

## Executive Summary

Kit Digital — Spain's flagship SME digitalization subsidy funded by NextGenerationEU — is the single most relevant program for this analysis, and the competitive intelligence is clear: **KinderClose is already an accredited Agente Digitalizador**, meaning they actively use Kit Digital as a sales tool. SocialKinder also references Kit Digital eligibility. The program itself closed to new applications on October 31, 2025, but a January 2026 BOE order creates a legal path for reactivation using unspent funds, with pressure from Brussels to execute before August 2026.

The key strategic implication: becoming an accredited Agente Digitalizador is not optional for competitive parity — KinderClose has made it a selling point, and schools in Spain already know the program. This needs to be planned for from the start, even though the startup does not yet meet the revenue threshold to qualify.

## 1. Kit Digital — Detailed Analysis

### 1.1 What Is It?

Kit Digital is a Spanish government program (managed by Red.es, part of the Ministry for Digital Transformation) that provides non-repayable "digital vouchers" (bonos digitales) to SMEs, microenterprises, and self-employed workers to fund the adoption of pre-approved digital solutions. It is financed by the EU's NextGenerationEU Recovery and Resilience Mechanism.

Total program spend: over €3.4 billion disbursed to approximately 850,000 businesses over three years — the largest digitalization subsidy in Spanish history.

### 1.2 Can Guarderías Apply? — Eligibility Analysis

**The short answer: private guarderías can apply. Public ones cannot.**

The eligibility criteria (BOE-A-2021-21873, the founding regulation) target:

- Small enterprises (10-49 employees) — Segment I
- Microenterprises (3-9 employees) — Segment II
- Microenterprises and self-employed (0-2 employees) — Segment III

The key gate is whether the entity is registered as an economic activity in the Tax Authority census (Censo de empresarios, profesionales y retenedores) under an IAE epigraph.

**For guarderías specifically:**

- **Private guarderías** (titularidad privada): registered under IAE epígrafe 931.1 ("Guardería y enseñanza de educación infantil, exclusivamente") or CNAE 8510 (Educación preprimaria) or CNAE 8891 (Actividades de guardería para niños). They operate as commercial businesses. **Eligible.**
- **Guarderías concertadas**: receive public funding but are privately operated. They typically maintain IAE registration. **Likely eligible**, though the concertada relationship adds complexity (they may receive other public subsidies that count toward de minimis limits).
- **Escuelas infantiles municipales / públicas**: owned by public administration, classified as public entities. **Explicitly excluded** from Kit Digital. Public entities and public law entities are not covered.

This is a clean distinction: your target market is private guarderías, which are exactly the eligible segment.

**Additional eligibility conditions for a school to qualify:**

- Must have been active for at least 6 months (recent update: was 2 years, reduced to 6 months under TDF/39/2026)
- Current on all tax and Social Security obligations
- Not classified as a company in crisis
- Total de minimis aid received in 3 fiscal years cannot exceed €300,000
- Must complete a self-diagnostic test on acelerapyme.es before applying

### 1.3 Segment Sizes and Subsidy Amounts

| Segment | Employee Count | Maximum Bono |
|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Segment I | 10–49 employees | €12,000 |
| Segment II | 3–9 employees | €6,000 |
| Segment III | 0–2 employees / self-employed | €2,000–€3,000 |
| Segments IV-V | 50–249 employees (medium) | €25,000–€29,000 |

Most independent guarderías fall in **Segment II (3-9 employees)** at €6,000, or **Segment I (10-49 employees)** at €12,000 for larger centers. A school with 3 teachers + 1 director is already Segment II.

The bono is a maximum total — schools can allocate it across multiple solution categories from multiple accredited providers.

### 1.4 Which Solution Category Fits a School-Parent Communication App?

There are 14+ solution categories. The most relevant for this product are:

**Option A — Gestión de Procesos (Process Management)**
This is the category KinderClose uses. It covers digitalization of business/operational processes: administration, scheduling, billing, notifications, client management. Maximum subsidy per category varies by segment. This is the broadest and most defensible fit for an all-in-one guarderías management and communication tool.

**Option B — Gestión de Clientes (Customer Management / CRM)**
Covers tools that centralize client (in this case: families) information, track interactions, and manage relationships. Maximum: €4,000–€14,000 depending on segment and whether AI is included. A communication and messaging app between school and families could be positioned here.

**Option C — Comunicaciones Seguras (Secure Communications)**
Covers secure email, messaging, video conferencing, and corporate communication tools. Maximum: up to €29,000 for larger segments. A pure school-parent messaging app could potentially qualify here, but this category is more typically claimed for infrastructure-level comms tools (corporate email, VoIP).

**The recommended positioning: Gestión de Procesos.** This is the category precedent set by KinderClose. It positions the app as a center administration and family engagement platform, which is broader and more defensible than pure communications. Many school-focused EdTech platforms (Clickedu, Educamos, SocialKinder) also cluster around this category.

**What the subsidy covers in practice:**
The bono pays the accredited provider directly. Schools do not receive cash — they sign a service agreement with the Agente Digitalizador, who then claims the subsidy from Red.es and receives payment. The school typically pays little or nothing upfront for the contracted period (usually 12 months), with the subsidy covering installation + first year of service.

KinderClose, for reference, priced their Kit Digital offering at €1,500 + VAT for installation and 12 months of maintenance — well within the available subsidy envelope.

### 1.5 Program Status in 2026 — Critical Nuance

**Current status: closed to new applications, with a legally enabled pathway to reopen.**

Key timeline:
- October 31, 2025: All Kit Digital convocations officially closed. No new applications accepted.
- January 28, 2026: BOE publishes Orden TDF/39/2026, which modifies the regulatory framework to:
  - Remove fixed expiration dates (program now runs "until funds are exhausted")
  - Mandate reinvestment of any unexecuted funds back into Kit Digital rather than returning to Brussels
  - Enable Red.es to automatically re-award funds from withdrew/failed applications to previously rejected applicants without new submissions
- April 2026 (now): No new convocation has been announced. However, the European Commission requires Spain to have resolved its NextGenerationEU aids by August 2026, creating strong institutional pressure to reactivate before summer.

**Bottom line on status:** Schools that have not yet applied cannot currently do so. If a new convocation opens (probable, but not confirmed), the window will be narrow — potentially closing again before August 2026. For a startup in validation phase today, the relevant scenario is: by the time you have a product and your first paying schools, a new Kit Digital round may or may not be available. Plan for both scenarios.

### 1.6 The Agente Digitalizador Requirement

**This is the most important structural constraint for the startup.**

Schools do not apply for Kit Digital and then choose software. The process is:

1. School applies for a bono digital at sede.red.gob.es
2. Red.es approves the bono (2 weeks to 4 months)
3. School must spend the bono within 6 months of approval
4. School can only spend the bono with accredited Agentes Digitalizadores listed in the official Red.es catalog
5. School signs an agreement with the Agente Digitalizador
6. Agente Digitalizador delivers the service and then claims payment from Red.es

**You cannot benefit from Kit Digital as a vendor unless you are an accredited Agente Digitalizador.**

KinderClose is accredited. SocialKinder references the program. Clickedu, Educamos, and Additio are all accredited or in process. Any school that wants to use their Kit Digital bono to pay for your app can only do so if you are on the official catalog.

**Requirements to become an Agente Digitalizador:**

- Revenue threshold: minimum €100,000 accumulated in the two years prior to application, OR €50,000 in the immediately preceding year — all from projects equivalent to the solutions you intend to offer
- For self-employed without employees: €70,000 over two years or €35,000 in the prior year
- Revenue must be from the Spanish market
- Must be current on taxes and Social Security
- Must not be classified as a company in crisis
- Must have fiscal domicile or service delivery center in the EU
- Must maintain a dedicated Kit Digital page on your own domain listing solutions by segment

**The revenue threshold is a real constraint for a pre-revenue startup.** A company with zero revenue today cannot become an Agente Digitalizador until it has at least €50,000 in annual revenue (or €100,000 over two years) from comparable projects. This realistically means Kit Digital accreditation is a Year 2 or Year 3 milestone, not Day 1.

**Tactical workaround while you cannot be accredited yourself:**
Partner with an existing Agente Digitalizador who resells or white-labels your solution under their accreditation. This is done in the market — some Agentes Digitalizadores act as intermediaries for third-party software. The economics are less favorable (the partner takes a cut), but it allows schools to use their bono for your app before you meet the revenue threshold. This should be explored with existing accredited players in the education tech space.

## 2. Other National Programs

### 2.1 Kit Consulting

A companion program to Kit Digital, also managed by Red.es and funded by NextGenerationEU. Instead of funding software implementation, it funds strategic consulting to design digital transformation roadmaps.

- Targets: SMEs from 10 to 249 employees
- Amounts: €12,000 (Segment A: 10-49 employees), €18,000 (Segment B: 50-99), €24,000 (Segment C: 100-249)
- Services: AI roadmaps, cybersecurity audits, full digital transformation strategy

**Relevance for guarderías:** Limited direct relevance — most guarderías fall below the 10-employee threshold for this program. However, a larger guarderías group or chain might qualify. The program also signals what's coming next in the government's digitalization agenda.

### 2.2 PYME Digital (Cámaras de Comercio)

The Chambers of Commerce (Cámaras de Comercio) run digitalization programs for SMEs. Availability varies by region and year. These typically fund action plans rather than SaaS subscriptions, but they sometimes include software implantation as an eligible cost. Less directly relevant than Kit Digital, and funding amounts are smaller.

## 3. Regional Programs

### 3.1 Comunidad de Madrid — Ayudas a la Digitalización de Pymes 2026

The Comunidad de Madrid activated a new digitalization subsidy program in 2026 under its FEDER Operational Programme 2021-2027:

- **Amount:** 50% subsidy on eligible investments, up to €10,000 per applicant
- **Minimum investment:** €5,000 for service sector businesses
- **Who qualifies:** SMEs and self-employed in commerce, services, and artisanal sectors in Madrid
- **Eligible costs:** "Implantation of technological solutions and digital tools" for business process improvement, IT equipment, management software, customer service systems
- **Deadline:** March 24, 2026 (this has already passed as of the research date)
- **Managed by:** Comunidad de Madrid (sede.comunidad.madrid)

This program was open to guarderías in Madrid registered as private businesses. The deadline has passed for the current round, but FEDER-backed programs typically repeat annually or biennially.

**Actionable note:** Monitor for the 2027 round of this or equivalent Madrid programs. The March 2026 round had a clear services-sector eligibility that would include private guarderías.

### 3.2 Catalunya — FEDER Digitalization Grants

Catalonia runs digitalization grants under its own FEDER programs. The search results show a 40% subsidy rate for technology innovation projects. Specific programs for small service businesses exist but require monitoring through the Generalitat's Departament d'Empresa i Treball. The Catalan market is also relevant given the presence of llar d'infants (0-3 private childcare) which are actively sought as school candidates.

### 3.3 País Vasco / Euskadi

- The Basque government has a major digital education strategy called ADIMEN DIGITALA / IRADI with €271.6 million committed through 2029, but this targets the public education system, not private guarderías
- SPRI (Basque Agency for Business Development) runs innovation and digitalization grants for private businesses, but these skew toward manufacturing and industrial sectors
- No specific digitalization program for private guarderías was found for 2025-2026

### 3.4 Andalucía

Andalucía TRADE offers up to 75% subsidy on eligible digitalization investment for industrial development projects — but this is oriented toward manufacturing and industrial firms, not service businesses. General FEDER programs exist but were not found to have open 2026 rounds specifically targeting small service businesses like guarderías.

### 3.5 EU Direct Funds — Erasmus+ and European Social Fund

**Erasmus+ (education focus):** Primarily funds school-to-school partnerships, teacher training, and curriculum development — not software procurement. Not directly relevant.

**European Social Fund (ESF+) 2021-2027:** Channeled through comunidades autónomas for workforce development and inclusion. Not a fit for SaaS procurement.

**Conclusion on EU direct funds:** No EU program directly funds SaaS procurement by private guarderías. EU money reaches guarderías through national/regional programs like Kit Digital, not through direct grants.

## 4. Sales Implications

### 4.1 Does Kit Digital Change the Sales Conversation?

Yes, significantly — in both directions.

**When Kit Digital is available and you are accredited:**
- The budget objection ("we can't afford €59/month") is largely neutralized for the first year
- Schools that have already received their bono and not yet spent it are highly motivated to use it quickly (6-month clock is ticking)
- The conversation shifts from "can you afford this?" to "have you used your Kit Digital bono yet?" — a much easier framing
- KinderClose actively uses this framing. Their Kit Digital page is part of their sales funnel.

**Current situation (April 2026 — program closed):**
- Schools that already received and spent their bono on another solution: irrelevant
- Schools that received a bono and haven't used it yet: possible, but the 6-month window may have expired
- Schools that never applied: cannot currently get a new bono
- If a new convocation opens before August 2026: schools could apply and then be motivated to find providers quickly

**The medium-term picture:**
Plan for Kit Digital as a sales accelerator from Year 2 onward, once you are accredited. In Year 1 (validation and first school cohort), use it as a conversation point — "we're in the process of becoming an accredited Agente Digitalizador, so you'll be able to use your next Kit Digital bono for our platform" — without over-promising.

### 4.2 The Buying Process When Kit Digital Is Involved

When a new convocation is open and you are accredited, the process from the school's perspective is:

1. School does the self-diagnostic test on acelerapyme.es (10-15 minutes)
2. School applies at sede.red.gob.es — requires digital certificate or Cl@ve identification
3. Red.es approves the bono — **2 weeks to 4 months** (typically 2-6 weeks in practice)
4. School has **6 months** to sign an agreement with an accredited Agente Digitalizador
5. School signs the service agreement with you (the Agente Digitalizador)
6. You deliver the solution and invoice Red.es
7. Red.es pays you directly (typically within 60-90 days of justification submission)

From the school's perspective, they sign and start using the software with zero upfront cost. You carry the float until Red.es pays, which has been a pain point for smaller Agentes Digitalizadores in previous rounds.

**Total elapsed time from school deciding to start: 4-8 weeks before they have an active account.**

This is longer than a standard SaaS signup, but schools are used to slow procurement cycles. More importantly, the decision is essentially irreversible once the bono is committed — reducing churn risk in Year 1.

### 4.3 Pricing Strategy Implications

KinderClose priced their Kit Digital offering at **€1,500 + VAT for year one** (installation + 12 months), which sits well within Segment II's €6,000 bono. This is substantially below your planned €59/month (€708/year) pricing — they packaged a higher-value offering to capture more of the subsidy.

When you become accredited, you have two strategic options:

- **Option A — Subsidy-priced package:** Offer a "Kit Digital package" priced at €1,500-€2,500 + VAT covering onboarding + 2 years of service, positioned to use the full Segment III bono (€2,000-€3,000) or a meaningful portion of Segment II's €6,000. The school pays nothing.
- **Option B — Standard pricing, Kit Digital as payment method:** Maintain €59/month pricing, allow the bono to cover 10-12 months, then transition to standard recurring payment. Simpler commercially.

Option B aligns better with your recurring revenue model and avoids discounting in the critical early acquisition phase.

### 4.4 Caveats and Risks

**Accreditation revenue threshold is a blocker in Year 1.** You need €50,000+ in relevant project revenue in the year before applying, or €100,000 over two years. Until you hit that threshold, you cannot be accredited. Plan for accreditation as a Q3/Q4 2027 milestone (after 12+ months of paying schools).

**Kit Digital may not reopen.** The January 2026 BOE order enables reopening but does not guarantee it. If the EU's August 2026 deadline passes without a new convocation, the program may be over for this funding cycle entirely. Do not build the business plan around Kit Digital as a required channel.

**Schools that received bonos may have already spent them.** The first convocations opened in 2022. Many schools already received and spent their bonos — they cannot receive a second bono under the current rules (one per entity). A new convocation would need to explicitly allow new applicants or previously excluded entities.

**Concertadas may have de minimis complications.** Concertadas already receive public funding for operations. While they can still access Kit Digital, their existing public aid counts toward the €300,000 de minimis ceiling. This is not usually a practical barrier for €59/month software, but it's worth noting.

**The float risk.** As an Agente Digitalizador, you deliver the service and then wait for Red.es to pay. In Kit Digital rounds, payment delays of 3-6 months were common. This is a cash flow consideration at scale, not at first-cohort size.

## 5. Competitive Intelligence on KinderClose and Subsidy Use

KinderClose has a dedicated "Agente Digitalizador" page on their website and actively promotes Kit Digital eligibility to schools. Their positioning:

- They are accredited under the **Gestión de Procesos** category
- They priced their Kit Digital offering at **€1,500 + VAT / year** (covering installation + 12 months)
- They frame the pitch as: "Los Centros de Educación Infantil pueden solicitar una subvención Kit Digital para instalar KinderClose" — the subsidy is the lead, not the product
- Over 500 centers in Spain already use KinderClose

SocialKinder also explicitly states they are a "homologated solution within the Kit-Digital program" and references NextGenerationEU funds. Their accreditation status is less clear from public information.

**Strategic implication:** The Kit Digital angle is table stakes in this market segment. Schools know about it. The absence of Kit Digital accreditation will come up in sales calls, especially for any school that has not yet used their bono. You need a clear answer ready: "We are in the process of accreditation — timeline is Q3 2027 — and in the meantime we offer a 30-day money-back guarantee and can provide documentation to support your application with another accredited partner."

## 6. Recommended Actions by Priority

**Immediate (validation phase, now):**
1. Add a question to the Mom Test call script: "Have you used your Kit Digital bono? Is it spent?" — this is useful discovery data about market saturation and remaining opportunity
2. When schools ask about Kit Digital, be honest: "We're building toward accreditation — it's a Year 2 milestone once we meet the revenue threshold."
3. Note in the school-candidates CSV whether each school mentions Kit Digital awareness or has already used it

**Medium term (first paying schools, Year 1):**
4. Research existing accredited Agentes Digitalizadores in the education tech space who might resell or partner with your solution. A referral/white-label arrangement could let early schools use their bonos through a partner while you build toward your own accreditation.
5. Track the Red.es portal and BOE for any new Kit Digital convocation announcement before August 2026.

**Year 2 milestone:**
6. Once you have €50,000+ in annual ARR from comparable projects, apply for Agente Digitalizador accreditation. Prepare the dedicated Kit Digital page on your domain in advance.
7. Position "Kit Digital accepted" prominently on the website and in sales materials once accredited.

**Ongoing:**
8. Monitor Madrid FEDER digitalization programs (new round expected 2027) as a secondary subsidy channel for Madrid-based guarderías.
9. Watch for Catalunya-specific FEDER programs as the second-largest target market.
